The Foundations: Slavery and Its Immediate Aftermath 1619 - First documented arrival of enslaved Africans in English North America (Virginia) 1662 - Virginia statute establishes that children's status (enslaved or free) follows the mother, encoding slavery into law and inheritance 1705 - Virginia Slave Codes consolidate earlier laws, establishing comprehensive legal framework for slavery 1793 - First Fugitive Slave Act passed, requiring return of escaped enslaved people 1831 - Nat Turner's Rebellion leads to intensified surveillance and control systems across the South 1850 - Fugitive Slave Act strengthened, federal enforcement of slavery expanded 1857 - Dred Scott v.
