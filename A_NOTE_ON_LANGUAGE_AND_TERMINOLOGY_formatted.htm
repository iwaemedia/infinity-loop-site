Racial Terminology This book uses"Black" (capitalized) to refer to people of African descent in the United States, following the contemporary scholarly and journalistic consensus that recognizes Black as both a racial and cultural identity deserving of the capitalization typically afforded to other ethnic and national identities. The book uses"white" (lowercase) to refer to people of European descent, following the convention that whiteness as a social category functions differently than other racial identities and represents a position of structural power rather than a cultural or ethnic heritage. Historical sources quoted in this book often use terminology that is now considered offensive or outdated, including"Negro,""colored," and worse. These terms are preserved in direct quotations to maintain historical accuracy and to document the ideological framing of different eras. Their use in quotation does not indicate endorsement. The Term"Infinity Loop" The central metaphor and analytical framework of this book is the"Infinity Loop," a term chosen to capture three essential characteristics of the system documented here: Self-perpetuating circularity: The system feeds back into itself, with each mechanism enabling the next in an unbroken cycle. Adaptive persistence: Like a mathematical infinity, the system continues indefinitely, regenerating across technological, legal, and ideological transformations. Dimensional complexity: The infinity symbol (∞) suggests multiple interconnected loops operating simultaneously, much as the four mechanisms of the Loop interact and reinforce one another. This is an analytical framework, not a conspiracy theory. The Loop does not require centralized coordination or conscious intent by all actors. It operates through institutional logics, economic incentives, and cultural narratives that align individual and organizational behavior toward a consistent systemic outcome."Control" vs."Oppression" This book deliberately uses the term"control" rather than"oppression" as its primary analytical lens. While oppression accurately describes the lived experience of those targeted by the Loop,"control" more precisely captures the system's functional purpose and operational logic. The Loop is not primarily about inflicting suffering for its own sake (though it does inflict immense suffering). It is about managing, monitoring, and extracting from a population defined as requiring containment. This distinction matters for strategy: systems of control have specific mechanisms that can be identified, analyzed, and dismantled."Abolition" and the Abolitionist Framework This book concludes with an explicitly abolitionist argument, drawing on the framework developed by prison abolition scholars and activists. Abolition, in this context, does not mean merely the elimination of prisons and police (though that is part of the vision). It refers to the dismantling of the entire carceral logic and the building of life-affirming institutions in its place. The book distinguishes between: Reform: Changes within the existing system that may improve conditions but leave the fundamental architecture intact Resistance: Opposition to the system's operation, survival within and despite its mechanisms Abolition: The systematic dismantling of the carceral machinery and the creation of new social arrangements"Carceral" and"Carceral State""Carceral" refers to systems and logics of imprisonment and confinement. The"carceral state" encompasses not only prisons and jails but the entire apparatus of surveillance, policing, courts, probation, parole, and associated bureaucracies that function to monitor, control, and confine. Statistical and Quantitative Language This book presents extensive quantitative data. Where possible, racial disparities are presented as ratios (e. G.,"Black Americans are arrested at 5 times the rate of white Americans") rather than as percentages, as ratios more clearly communicate the magnitude of disparity. Statistical claims are sourced to Tier 1 government data or Tier 2 peer-reviewed studies except where otherwise noted. Legal Citations Legal sources are cited using standard legal citation format. Statutes are identified by jurisdiction, code section, and year. Court cases are identified by case name, jurisdiction, and year. This allows readers to locate and verify the primary legal sources that document the Loop's operation. Methodological Transparency Throughout this book, I have attempted to maintain methodological transparency, clearly identifying the strength and limitations of the evidence supporting each claim. Where data is incomplete or contested, this is noted. Where the evidentiary record contains gaps (and it does), these gaps are acknowledged. The goal is not to create a seamless narrative that obscures complexity, but to provide an honest accounting of what the evidence shows and what remains uncertain. The historical record is vast and uneven. Some periods are thoroughly documented in government archives and scholarly studies. Others remain underexamined, their evidence scattered across local records, personal testimonies, and newspaper accounts. This book synthesizes the available evidence while acknowledging that much remains to be documented and understood. A Living Framework The Infinity Loop is an analytical framework, not a fixed dogma. It is offered as a lens through which to understand persistent patterns, a tool for organizing evidence and identifying systemic connections. Like any analytical framework, it will be refined, challenged, and developed by future scholarship and activism. This book documents the pattern as it has operated from 1865 to 2025. The work of resistance and abolition will write the next chapters.
